# Report Template

Fill every section. Keep prose dense but readable. Use tables for competitors
and scenarios. Replace `{Company}` throughout. Omit a section only if it is
genuinely not applicable, and say why.

---

# {Company} — Deep Dive & Assessment

## 1. The one-paragraph version
A single plain-English paragraph a smart non-expert fully understands. What
the company does, for whom, and why it is different — no jargon, no acronyms
left unexpanded. Include the company's own tagline if it has one.

## 2. What they actually do (in depth)
Concrete product surfaces: each offering, who it is for, pricing if known,
interfaces/SDKs, what a customer literally gets. Call out scope limits and
deliberate constraints (e.g. "open models only", "US-only", "design partners
only"). No marketing language — describe the thing.

## 3. How the technology / business mechanism works
From first principles, as if teaching a beginner. If the edge is technical,
explain the underlying mechanism and *why* it is hard. If the edge is
distribution, GTM, or data, explain that engine instead. End with the
one-sentence "so what" — what you actually have to trust/believe.

## 4. Founders & team — why (or whether) they're credible
Names, prior companies/labs/education, and the specific reason each is (or
isn't) the right person for this exact problem. State a verdict on team
strength.

## 5. The problem & why it matters
Why the status quo is unacceptable for the target buyer; the regulatory,
economic, or technical forcing function; how big and how urgent the pain
really is (distinguish headline TAM from the realistic must-have segment).

## 6. Competitors
A table: Category | Players | How they differ from {Company}. Cover direct
startups, incumbents/hyperscalers/platforms, "good enough" substitutes, and
any architectural benchmark that is not actually a competitor.

## 7. The moat — and how durable it is
State plainly what is and is **not** defensible. Name the real moat
(technology vs. brand/trust vs. distribution vs. data vs. switching costs vs.
regulatory). Give a durability rating (fragile / moderate / strong) with the
reason.

## 8. Strengths & weaknesses
Two crisp bulleted lists. Weaknesses must be real and specific (capital,
team size, dependency risk, sales cycle, scope limits) — not softened.

## 9. Where they'll likely be in 3 years (scenarios)
Explicitly labeled informed speculation. Bull / Base / Bear, each with a
rough probability and a one-line trigger. End with **the single biggest
variable** the outcome hinges on.

## 10. Overall assessment
An opinionated verdict in 1–2 short paragraphs: is the thesis sound, is the
team A-grade, is the position structurally easy or hard, and what is the most
likely real-world outcome. If framed as an investment, state what to push
hardest on in diligence.

---

**Confidence notes:** What is confirmed from primary sources vs. inferred vs.
unverified. Explicitly flag any money/traction numbers sourced from
third-party aggregators as low-confidence. Note namesake/disambiguation
issues. List anything you could not verify.

**Want me to go deeper?** Offer a short menu (e.g. technical teardown,
one-page investment memo, competitor-by-competitor matrix). Do not pre-write
these.
